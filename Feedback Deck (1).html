Section 01 · Global / Header
Header & navigation
1 · STANDINGS wraps to 2 lines
2 · Heavy pill
4 · Orphan gear icon
1
Nav items inconsistent height. "Standings" wraps because the emoji sits above the label while others are single-line. The row grows taller than it needs to.Fix: nav button{white-space:nowrap;display:inline-flex;align-items:center;gap:6px;line-height:1}
2
Active nav pill is heavier than the accent intends. Solid orange fill on "Home" out-shouts content. Soften to a low-alpha background + orange text, or a bottom border.
3
Header's bottom border is loud at 3px. Try 2px — the team gradient is already doing the work.
4
Settings icon feels orphaned. Tiny gear with a 1px border next to full-size nav pills looks like a different kit. Match height/padding, or separate it with a visual gap.

Section 02 · Home
The rainbow gradient is doing too much
5 · 5-stop rainbow ★
6 · Badge stack
7 · "vs" is ghost-like
8 · Italic footnote ★
9 · Dark-on-dark rows
5
Five-stop gradient is busy. Away-primary → away-secondary → #111827 → home-secondary → home-primary creates a rainbow streak that dominates Home.Fix: 3 stops (away-primary → neutral 50% → home-primary), or drop the gradient to ~0.6 opacity over solid card bg so it reads as a tint.
6
TODAY and UPCOMING badges stack awkwardly. Move TODAY into the kicker and keep UPCOMING as the pill — or drop one.
7
"vs" is tiny and ghost-like. For upcoming games it's the whole story — give it more weight (1rem, 0.75 alpha).
8
Series record footnote is italic, small, low-contrast. Important context nearly invisible. Drop italic, bump to .8rem, raise contrast.
9
Next Series rows are dark-on-dark. rgba(0,0,0,.25) over the gradient obscures time/status text. Bump to .4 or add a 1px inner border.

Section 07 & 08 · News + Live Game
Uniform rhythm; competing attention-grabbers
24
News items all identical visual weight. 8px orange dot + title + meta for 20 headlines scans as a wall. Lead item gets slightly larger treatment (1rem → 1.05rem, no dot). Creates visual rhythm.
25
Live header has 3 attention-grabbers. Gradient bg + 2px orange border + red live badge all compete. Pick one — drop either the border or the gradient.
26
Live score block too close horizontally. Team scores 3rem + team names 1.1rem share a 20px gap. Bump to 48px — let scores anchor visually.
27
Count dots all same shape. 14px round for balls/strikes/outs — hard to distinguish at a glance. Different shape per type = faster read.
28
Live header text doesn't use --accent-text — invisible scores on some team themes. Already in your known issues; worth doing.
29
Linescore header uses --blue while other live cards use --orange kickers. Pick one.
LIVE HEADER — 3 COMPETING ELEMENTS
① Gradient background (team colors)
② 2px solid orange border
③ Pulsing red LIVE badge
Pick one. The LIVE badge is already the strongest signal — let the surrounding frame be quiet.

balls — circle (neutral)
strikes — square (rising)
outs — larger circle (terminal)

Section 09 · Cross-cutting
Consistency, theming, first-paint
30
Emoji in nav feel casual for a data-dense app and render differently per OS. Either commit to emoji everywhere (card titles already mix: 🔥 🃏 ⚾ 🥎 🧤) or swap nav to Unicode geometric marks / icon font. Inconsistency is the real issue.
31
.card-title is orange + uppercase + tracked — great for one or two per view, but Stats tab has 10+. Use orange only for section titles, muted for sub-card titles.
32
First-paint Mets flash on theme-switched reload. :root defines Mets defaults; applyTeamTheme overrides on JS init. Users with localStorage set to another team see Mets orange for a frame.Fix: hydrate :root from localStorage synchronously before first paint, OR keep root neutral and paint on init.
33
Font stack is 'Segoe UI', system-ui, sans-serif. Segoe is Windows-only; system-ui covers Mac/Linux. Reorder: system-ui, -apple-system, 'Segoe UI', sans-serif so system-ui wins on modern browsers.
34
--muted: #8892a4 is quite blue. Fights warm-team themes (Giants orange, Orioles). Derive --muted from team hue at low chroma, same as --card / --border.
